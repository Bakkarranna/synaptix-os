## PSYCHOLOGICAL PRINCIPLES FOR ALL MARKETING

Every piece of content you create should deploy one or more of these:

### Cialdini's 7 Principles Applied to Agency Content

| Principle | Definition | Content Application |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Reciprocity** | People repay favors | Give away a real framework or checklist for free. "We audited 10 AI apps — here's the checklist we used." |
| **Commitment & Consistency** | People align with past actions | Start with a micro-yes: "Does production quality matter?" → Yes → "Then here's what most founders miss." |
| **Social Proof** | People follow peers | Hierarchy: Case study > Industry testimonial > Data > Logo wall. Show "companies like yours" getting results. |
| **Authority** | People follow experts | Credential the message: third-party recognition, media mentions, verified skill installs, real technical depth. |
| **Liking** | People say yes to those they like | "We" language. Show genuine understanding of their specific context. Be warmly professional. |
| **Scarcity** | Limited = valuable | Real scarcity: "We take 3 clients per quarter for deep work." Fake urgency destroys trust in B2B. |
| **Unity** | Shared identity | "We're both founders building something that matters." Identity language outperforms behavior framing 2:1. |

### The Hooked Model for Content Loops (Nir Eyal)

Your content should create a habit loop for your audience:

1. **Trigger** — External (LinkedIn post, tweet, email) → Internal (curiosity about agentic engineering)
2. **Action** — Lowest friction click (read the post, open the carousel)
3. **Variable Reward** — Specific insight they didn't expect (not generic advice)
4. **Investment** — They comment, share, subscribe → loads the next trigger

### Interrupt Patterns (Gary Halbert, Russell Brunson)

The most effective headlines/interrupts follow one of these patterns:

| Pattern | Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **4 U's** (copywriting gold standard) | Urgent + Unique + Ultra-specific + Useful | "The 3 vulnerabilities in your AI-built app that hackers find in 47 seconds" |
| **Problem-Agitate-Solution** | Name the pain, stir it, solve it | "Your vibe-coded MVP is a security incident waiting to happen. Here's why. And how to fix it." |
| **Before-After-Bridge** | Current reality → Desired state → Your offer | "Right now your site is an expensive business card. Imagine it generating leads while you sleep." |
| **Pattern Interrupt** | Break the mental script | "Stop vibe-coding your startup (unless you're okay with rebuilding in 6 months)" |

## LINKEDIN STRATEGY (Justin Welsh System)

### Why LinkedIn First
- Personal profiles reach 31% of followers vs. 2% for company pages
- LinkedIn drives 80% of all B2B social media leads
- B2B buyers are here — company pages are not

### The Content Engine

**One expensive problem. Every post orbits it.**

Your expensive problem: Founders built MVPs with AI tools, now they need production-grade quality without rebuilding from scratch.

**Post Format:**
```
[Hook — under 10 words, makes them stop]

[Blank line]

[Story/Context — 2-3 lines about a specific situation]

[Blank line]

[The insight — 4-6 lines of real value]

[Blank line]

[Soft CTA — question or "DM me if X"]
```

### Weekly Content Calendar

| Day | Format | Topic Angle |
|-----|--------|-------------|
| Monday | Text post | New insight about agentic engineering |
| Tuesday | Carousel/PDF | Step-by-step: "How to audit your AI-built app in 10 min" |
| Wednesday | Text post | Client story (Problem → Action → Result) |
| Thursday | Text post | Hot take / contrarian opinion |
| Friday | Carousel/PDF | Checklist or framework |

### The Justin Welsh Numbers
- Post 1x/day from personal profile
- 150-300 words per post (highest engagement length)
- 60-minute window after posting: respond to EVERY comment
- Newsletter link in bio: convert readers to subscribers
- Monetize after trust is built

### Comment Strategy
- 15 comments/day on target prospects' posts
- Must be specific and valuable (3+ sentences)
- Don't comment "Great post!" — add a counterpoint, insight, or experience
- Do this BEFORE they know who you are

### What Formats Win in 2026
- **Carousel/PDF posts** — highest engagement at 6.60% (designed in Penpot)
- **Vertical video (<90 sec)** — 5x more engagement than static, underutilized in B2B
- **Text posts** — backbone of the strategy
- **LinkedIn Newsletters** — subscriber notification = free distribution

## CATEGORY DESIGN CONTENT (Lochhead's "Frame It, Name It, Claim It")

### Frame the Problem
Content that makes people see their problem in a new way:
- "Vibe coding isn't the problem. Not knowing when to productionize is."
- "Your MVP passed the demo. It won't pass a security audit."
- "Most agencies are selling you 2019 websites for 2026 prices."

### Name the Solution
Content that names the category you own:
- "Agentic Engineering is the new standard. Here's what it means."
- "Introducing the Vibe Code Audit — 48 hours to find the cracks."
- "Cinematic websites that convert. Engineered, not vibed."

### Claim the Position
Content that establishes authority:
- "We audited 10 AI-built apps last month. Here's what we found."
- "A technical breakdown of what Noomo's jellyfish costs vs. delivers."
- "The 5 questions every founder should ask before hiring a web agency."
