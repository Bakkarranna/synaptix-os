# SYNAPTIX STUDIO — COLD CALLING SCRIPTS (Sandler Method)

> **Framework:** Sandler Selling System v2
> **Core principle:** No pitching before qualifying. Period.
> **Goal of every call:** Book the discovery call. NOT close the sale.

---

## THE SANDLER MINDSET

Traditional sales: "I have a great product, let me tell you about it."
Sandler: "I have questions. Let me find out if there's a fit before either of us wastes time."

**Steli Efti (Close.com):** "Show up. Follow up. Close. 90% of selling is persistence, not persuasion."

## CHALLENGER SALE: Teaching Before Selling

Before any pitch, use commercial teaching to reframe their thinking.

### The Challenger Teach Structure (Matthew Dixon):

1. **Lead with Insight** — Tell them something they don't know about their own market:
   > "Most companies that built with Cursor or Lovable don't realize their codebase has 3 specific vulnerabilities that only appear once you hit real user traffic. We audited 10 AI-built apps last month — 8 had exposed API keys, 6 had disabled RLS. Would you like to see the breakdown?"

2. **Link to Their Specific Situation** — Connect the insight to their business:
   > "You just launched on ProductHunt. If you get the traffic you're aiming for, these issues go from theoretical to critical overnight."

3. **Create Constructive Tension** — Push them gently:
   > "Most agencies won't tell you this because they want the quick project. We're telling you because we'd rather build it right than fix it later."

4. **Guide to Your Solution** — Your offer becomes the obvious choice:
   > "That's why we built the Vibe Code Audit — 48 hours, prioritized fix list, before anything goes wrong."

### When to Use Challenger vs. Sandler

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Cold call, no prior relationship | Sandler — qualify first |
| Discovery call with qualified prospect | Challenger — teach then tailor |
| Prospect has been burned before | Sandler — build trust first |
| Prospect thinks they know the problem | Challenger — reframe their thinking |
| Decision-maker, high ACV deal | Challenger — take control |
| Multiple stakeholders involved | Challenger — tailor per stakeholder |

## STEP 1: UP-FRONT CONTRACT (Every Call Opens This Way)

This is the single most important technique. It replaces the old "generic opener."

```
"You:     Hi [Name], this is [You] from Synaptix Studio.
          I have 3 minutes.

          I build websites and AI systems for [industry] companies
          that actually work and convert.

          If we both agree there's a fit after 3 minutes, we'll
          book a 15-min call. If not, no hard feelings. Fair?"

[They say yes or ask what this is about]
```

**Why this works:** It disarms skepticism. You're not trying to "sell" them. You're qualifying each other. The prospect feels in control.

**Chris Voss tip:** After the contract, use a label: "It sounds like you've gotten calls like this before and most of them waste your time. That make sense?" This acknowledges their skepticism and makes them more open.

---

## STEP 2: PAIN DISCOVERY (The Sandler Pain Funnel)

Never pitch. Ask questions. The funnel:

```
Q1 (Surface):   "What's happening with [Company]'s website right now?"
                [They answer — listen. Don't jump in.]

Q2 (History):   "How long has that been going on?"
                [If they say "a while" — they're frustrated]

Q3 (Attempts):  "What have you tried to fix it so far?"
                [If nothing — they're stuck. If something — it failed.]

Q4 (Cost):      "What's that costing you in terms of leads or revenue?"
                [This is the key question. Quantified pain = real deal.]

Q5 (Urgency):   "If you don't fix this in the next 60-90 days,
                 what happens?"
                [Emotional weight. Future pain = urgency.]
```

**Do not pass Step 2 until you have a clear answer to Q4 and Q5.** If they can't articulate real pain with real cost, they won't buy. Move on.

---

## STEP 3: BUDGET (Before You Pitch)

Sandler rule: Never present a solution before you know the budget.

```
"You:     And if we found a way to fix this — what has your
          company invested in similar projects before?"

[They answer]

"You:     And for a project like this, what range were you
          thinking?"
```

**If they say "I don't know"** — that's a flag. Push gently:
> "Just ballpark — under $5K? Between $5-15K? Over $15K?"

**If they say "I need to think about it"** — they're not qualified yet. Revisit pain.

---

## STEP 4: DECISION PROCESS (Prevent Ghosting)

Before you book ANY next step, know:

```
"You:     Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"

"You:     What does your decision process look like?"

"You:     What timeline are you working with?"
```

**If they say "I need to run it by my partner/boss/board":**
> "Makes sense. Let's get them on the next call too. When can we do that?"

**If they say "I can decide on my own":**
> "Great. So if we find a fit today, you're good to move forward?"

---

## STEP 5: FULFILLMENT (Only Now Do You Pitch)

Keep it under 60 seconds:

```
"Here's what I'd recommend based on what you told me:

[1 sentence summarizing their pain]
[1 sentence on the solution]
[1 sentence on expected outcome]

Worth getting on a 15-minute call this week to go deeper?"
```

## COMPLETE CALL SCRIPTS

### Script A: Targeted Call (Referral / Known Lead)

```
UP-FRONT CONTRACT:
"Hi [Name], [You] from Synaptix. [Mutual contact] suggested I reach out.
I have 3 minutes. I help [industry] companies with [their type of problem].

After 3 minutes, if we both see a fit, we book a deeper call.
If not, no hard feelings. Fair?"

PAIN FUNNEL:
"Quick question — when someone finds [Company] online,
what do you want them to do? Is that happening right now?"
[Let them answer]
"How long has that been the case?"
"What have you tried so far?"
"What's that costing you in terms of revenue/leads?"

BUDGET:
"And if we fixed this — what range have you invested in before?"

DECISION:
"Who else needs to be on board with this decision?"

CLOSE:
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I'd do:
[Pain] → [Solution] → [Outcome]

I have Tuesday 2pm or Thursday 10am for a 15-min walkthrough.
Which works?"
```

### Script B: Targeted Call (Trigger-Based — Funding / New Hire / Launch)

```

PAIN:
```

## OBJECTION HANDLING (Sandler + Voss + Gap Selling)

Sandler says: objections are not obstacles — they're buying signals.

| Objection | Sandler Response | Chris Voss / Gap Selling Add-on |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| "How much?" | "Great question — I want to make sure we're in the right ballpark first. Can I ask what you've invested?" | **Mirror:** "Invested in similar projects before?" (pause, let them answer) |
| "Send me your portfolio" | "Happy to. But to send the most relevant examples — what's the one thing you need differently?" | **Label:** "Sounds like you need to see proof before investing. Totally fair." |
| "I'll think about it" | "Of course. What specifically? Budget? Timing?" | **Calibrated Q:** "What would need to be true for you to move forward?" |
| "We already have someone" | "Are you happy with the results?" | **Gap Sell:** "What would they say needs to improve? What's the gap between what you have and what you want?" |
| "Too expensive" | "Compared to what? What's the cost of NOT fixing this?" | **Gap Sell:** "Let's map the current cost. How many leads are you losing per month right now?" |
| "Not right now" | "When would be better? What needs to change?" | **Label:** "Sounds like timing, not interest. What's the trigger that would make this urgent?" |

### Chris Voss Mirroring Technique
When you hear an objection, pause, then repeat the last 1-3 words as a question:
- "We don't have the budget." → "Don't have the budget?" (they'll elaborate)
- "We're happy with our current setup." → "Happy with it?" (they'll admit what's not working)

### Gap Selling: Amplifying the Cost of Inaction (Keenan)

When they say "not now" or "I'll think about it":

```
Q: "If nothing changes, where will you be in 12 months?"
→ They describe the same problem, worse

Q: "What's that going to cost you by then?"
→ Quantified future pain

Q: "What's the gap between where you are and where you could be?"
→ They articulate the delta

Q: "Is closing that gap worth [your price] if we can do it in [timeline]?"
→ They sell themselves
```

If they can't articulate a clear gap with real cost, they won't buy regardless of your pitch.

### The Sandler "Negative Reverse":
> "You know what — based on what you've shared, maybe this isn't the right fit. What would need to be true for it to make sense?"

This paradoxically makes them defend the possibility because it removes pressure.

## STELI EFTI'S FOLLOW-UP RULES

1. **Same day:** Email within 2 hours of call — reference ONE specific thing they said
2. **Day 3:** LinkedIn message — "Following up on our chat"
3. **Day 7:** Email — share relevant case study or article
4. **Day 10:** Call — "I sent over [X]. Did you get a chance to look?"
5. **Day 14:** Email — final follow-up
6. **Day 21:** LinkedIn — long-term nurture (like/comment on their posts)

**80% of deals close after 5+ touches. 44% of salespeople give up after 1.**
